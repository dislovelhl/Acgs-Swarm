\begin{abstract}
Multi-agent AI governance systems face a critical security challenge: when agents
broadcast their trust-routing matrices over a peer-to-peer network, adversarial nodes
can corrupt honest replicas or reverse-engineer agents' private internal state.
We present \fedsink{} (\textbf{Fed}erated \textbf{Sink}horn with
\textbf{M}ulti-\textbf{A}gent \textbf{C}onstitutional \textbf{I}ntegrity), a
Byzantine-resilient federated governance protocol combining four mechanisms:
(1) Merkle-CRDT gossip for eventually consistent distributed state;
(2) spectral-sphere projection with residual injection to prevent trust collapse;
(3) Gaussian $(\varepsilon, \delta)$-differential privacy over broadcast routing matrices;
and (4) zk-SNARK proofs (MACI) to prevent bribery attacks.

We prove three security properties.
\textbf{CID Integrity}: a Byzantine node cannot inject a modified matrix that passes
honest-replica verification, because the content-identifier (CID) is bound to the
zk-SNARK proof.
\textbf{Eventual Epistemic Convergence (EEC)}: all honest replicas converge to
identical governance state in $O(\log N)$ gossip rounds despite up to $\lfloor N/3
\rfloor$ Byzantine nodes.
\textbf{$(\varepsilon, \delta)$-DP Privacy}: agent trust weights satisfy differential
privacy with noise $\sigma = 2(1-\alpha)r\sqrt{2\ln(1.25/\delta)}/\varepsilon$; residual
injection at $\alpha{=}0.1$ reduces required noise by $10\%$ relative to the
non-residual baseline.

We evaluate \fedsink{} on a synthetic $N$-agent swarm benchmark and — upon completion
of Phase 3/4 experiments — on SWE-bench coding tasks.
The protocol is implemented in the \texttt{constitutional\_swarm} package
(constitutional hash \texttt{608508a9bd224290}).
\end{abstract}
